Choosing a Graphics Card

Choosing a graphics card that is right for you.

Upgrading your graphics card is actually really easy. Hopefully, this guide will help you to feel confident about installing a new graphics card, even if it's your very first time. MSI provides an easy upgrade guide for you to choose the right graphics card, which is a great place to go if you're unsure of where to start looking. Before you start getting ready for the journey, the first thing you should do is check your current hardware information:
  1. Check compatibility with your power supply: confirm the output (wattage and amperage) that your power supply is capable of, and compare this to the power requirements for the graphics card you are looking for. Sites like EVGA's Power Meter can help you determine compatibility between parts as well.
  2. Check compatibility with your case: graphics cards vary in length, and depending on the size of your case and where the graphics card is able to be mounted, some cards may be too large. Check the measurements in your chosen graphics card's descriptions to confirm that they will fit in your case.

Once you've found a card that is compatible with your power supply and case, you're ready to order!
