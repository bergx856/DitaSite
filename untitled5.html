Installing a Graphics Card

Installing a graphics card safely and correctly.

For the upgrading process, you'll need a Phillips-head screwdriver and a bit of time. Be sure to turn off your PC and unplug all the cables before you begin.

  1. Remove the side panel from your case.
  2. Unplug the power connector from the old graphics card.
  3. Remove the screws that hold the graphics card in place at the back of your case.
  4. Make sure that you release the small plastic latch on the end of the PCI-E slot on your motherboard to unlock your old graphics card.
  5. Gently lift the old graphics card out of the case.

With the old graphics card removed, it's time to install your new graphics card.

  1. Carefully plug the new graphics card into the PCI-E slot on your motherboard. The plastic latch will close automatically with a "click" sound when the card is fully secured in place.
  2. Use the case screws to secure the graphics card's metal retention bracket to the back of your case.
  3. Connect the required number of 6-pin or 8-pin power connector from your power supply unit to your new card. If your new graphics card doesn't have any power connectors, it will get all the power it needs from the motherboard.
  4. Place the side panel back to close your PC.
  5. Connect your graphics card to your monitor by plugging in the video display cable to each.
  6. Turn on your computer.
  7. Download the latest drivers and software for your operating system from your card manufacturer's website.
